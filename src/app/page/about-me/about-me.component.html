Discover My Hometown - Chi-Phat is a remarkable community in the Cardamom Mountains, Southeast Asia’s largest mainland rainforest. Known for its emerald forests, estuaries, and mangroves, this region is a biodiversity hotspot and home to rare wildlife, including wild elephants, Siamese crocodiles, and Royal Turtles.

Chi-Phat commune has four villages—Chi-Phat, Komlot, Chom-Sla, and T'k La'o—with an estimated population of 3,000 (as of 2010). The residents primarily engage in agriculture, fishing, and tourism. The community’s ecotourism project, supported by Wildlife Alliance, has transformed Chi-Phat into a popular destination for both local and international visitors.

If you’re looking for an escape into nature, Chi-Phat offers breathtaking landscapes, exciting outdoor adventures, and a chance to connect with Cambodia’s rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re trekking through the jungle, kayaking in serene rivers, or enjoying the hospitality of the local community, Chi-Phat is an experience you’ll never forget.
